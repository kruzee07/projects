Exploring the Impact of Augmented Reality Interfaces on User Experience in HCI

University Course Project: COIS 3560H - Human Computer Interaction

Associated with Trent University
Mar 2024 - Apr 2024
Contributor(s): Samaksh Monga

This project investigates the impact of Augmented Reality (AR) interfaces on user experience (UX) within the realm of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). By examining how AR enhances user interactions by blending the physical and digital worlds, the research aims to understand its influence on usability, engagement, and accessibility across various sectors.

The project explores how AR is applied in fields such as safety, mobile navigation, personalized training, and language learning, highlighting its potential to transform industries like gaming, education, healthcare, and manufacturing. Through a combination of surveys, interviews, and usability testing, the project evaluates user preferences, challenges, and expectations, offering insights into both the strengths and limitations of current AR interfaces.

The goal of this research is to provide a deeper understanding of how AR can be integrated into user-centered designs, ultimately contributing to the development of more intuitive, engaging, and effective AR experiences.

SimPy vs GPSS: A Comparative Study in Modeling and Simulation

University Course Project: COIS 4470H - Modelling and Simulation

Associated with Trent University
Mar 2024 - Apr 2024
Contributor(s): Samaksh Monga

Key Highlights:

  • SimPy: A Python-based, open-source framework that offers flexibility and ease of use for simulating complex systems like queuing systems, inventory management, and healthcare models. It is designed for seamless integration with the extensive Python ecosystem.
  • GPSS: A long-established simulation language developed in the 1960s for discrete event modeling, especially in manufacturing and telecommunications. Known for its simplicity and efficiency in system modeling, GPSS excels at hierarchical modeling and predefined statistical functions.

Through hands-on experimentation, I analyzed the built-in features, implementation techniques, input/output mechanisms, and system efficiency of both tools. This analysis was supported by practical examples and visual demonstrations of each language's capabilities.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Comparative analysis of simulation frameworks
  • Hands-on modeling with both SimPy and GPSS
  • Creating simulation models for discrete event systems
  • Analyzing and presenting complex system behaviors through simulation results

This project provided valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each tool, offering a clearer perspective on when to use SimPy or GPSS based on specific simulation needs.

Hospital Information System (HIS) Upgrade Project

University Course Project: COIS 3030H - Software Specification and Requirements

Associated with Trent University
Oct 2023 - Dec 2023
Contributor(s): Samaksh Monga, Rahul Makhija, Ansh Adgaonkar

Objective: Modernize Peterborough Hospital's HIS to address inefficiencies, streamline operations, and improve patient care.

Key Contributions:

  • Problem Analysis: Identified critical challenges caused by a two-decade-old HIS, including manual triage, scheduling delays, and resource strain.
  • Goal Definition: Proposed automation of triage, scheduling, and insurance processes to reduce wait times and enhance operational efficiency.
  • Comprehensive Planning: Developed a complete requirements specification with detailed use case scenarios and 14 functional and non-functional user stories.
  • Measurable Outcomes: Defined metrics such as reduced patient wait times, improved emergency response, enhanced billing accuracy, and compliance with data security standards.
  • Target Timeline: Ensured the project plan aligns with the May 2024 deployment deadline for the upgraded HIS.

This project reflects my ability to manage large-scale system upgrades, align technical solutions with healthcare needs, and drive measurable outcomes.

Student and Alumni Job Outreach Database (JASS)

University Course Project: COIS 3400H - Database Management Systems

Associated with Trent University
Jun 2022 - Jul 2022

Objective:

Develop a scalable SQL database for connecting university students and alumni with employment opportunities.

Key Contributions:

  • Database Design: Created ER and UML diagrams, designing tables in 3NF/BCNF for efficient data organization and retrieval.
  • SQL Implementation: Wrote SQL scripts for table creation, sample data insertion, and common queries, such as locating alumni matching job profiles.
  • Automations: Developed triggers to handle automatic transitions (e.g., moving graduates to the alumni database) and job posting expirations.
  • Security: Built access control systems for different user levels, ensuring data protection and integrity.
  • Scalability & Backup: Proposed backup strategies and replication plans to accommodate large user bases, including potential scaling to over a million users.

This project demonstrates my expertise in database systems, automation, and problem-solving, tailored to meet organizational needs.

Vehicle System Forensics in the Digital Age

University Course Project: COIS 2750H - Computer Crime and Forensics

Associated with Trent University
Feb 2022 - Mar 2022

Objective: To analyze the role of digital forensics in modern vehicle systems and its implications for security, privacy, and investigation.

Key Highlights:

  • Autonomous Systems & Data: Explored how modern vehicle systems collect data (e.g., speed, location, braking) for operational efficiency and forensic purposes.
  • Forensic Tools: Studied tools like Berla Corporation's iVe, which extract data from black boxes and infotainment systems, aiding accident reconstruction and criminal investigations.
  • Privacy Challenges: Critiqued the risks of data misuse by unauthorized entities, especially regarding sensitive information like location and synced device logs.
  • Future Outlook: Examined the need for robust security measures in multi-layered vehicle architectures to mitigate vulnerabilities and protect users.

This paper demonstrates my ability to connect technological advancements with forensic applications, addressing both societal benefits and ethical concerns.
